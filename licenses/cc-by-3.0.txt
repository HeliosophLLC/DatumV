CC BY 3.0 is the predecessor to CC BY 4.0 and the dominant attribution license
for academic NLP corpora released between roughly 2008 and 2015 (including
SemEval task data, which TweetEval and its sentiment subset are derived from).
The two versions are similar in spirit — both allow commercial use with
attribution — but they are legally distinct documents. Datasets released under
CC BY 3.0 are usually upgraded to CC BY 4.0 only when a new release is cut, so
the older identifier persists.
